Waste Water Treatment
Summit
18th February 2016 Kingdom of Bahrain

The Bahrain water market is on the brink of dramatic changes. There is greater agreement within the sector that a change in policies, practices and processes is a necessity for water security, sustainability and well-being. The sector has demonstrated great creativity in the past months, developing new approaches to project financing and operations and rising to a range of challenges that it faces. For the industry to really thrive, however, it is critical that we can move beyond having to continuously reinvent the wheel. We need to create and adopt scalable solutions.

Cities around the world are challenged by water scarcity and deteriorated water resources. This demands treatment solutions able to improve water quality for cities facing population growth, urbanization, impacts of climate changes and a number of other global trends. Research institutions, organizations and public authorities have a strong tradition for cooperation. Common visions and cooperation on new, cutting-edge technologies that improve water quality is no exception.

Working in partnership with our network of senior executives we identify the key industry themes. These form the foundation of our summit and permeate every layer of the content-waste water program.

This is one of the biggest Summit organized to drive Bahrain by futuristic ways of water recycling. The Waste Water Treatment Summit is set to offer you an intimate environment for a focused discussion on new drivers shaping the future of Bahrain. The meetup will be combined with the lectures of eminent personalities and experts in the field of waste water from around the world. The presentations will focus on effective practices to benchmark and manage waste water generation/treatment in industries, municipalities and waste water generation sites.
